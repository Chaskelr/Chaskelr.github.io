Finding Range Estimates: Data Policy

Dear participant,

The research project “Finding Range Estimates” (conducted as part of the TRR 266 project B04) aims to investigate how people come up with and judge others’ range estimates. It is an experimental study conducted by:

Rico Chaskel

Humboldt University of Berlin

Unter den Linden 6, 10117 Berlin, Germany

Email: chaskelr@hu-berlin.de

In the following, I inform you about the handling of your personal data in accordance with the EU General Data Protection Regulation (EU GDPR). I kindly ask for your consent to participate in the experiment and to process your responses for the purposes specified. Please read the following information and the declaration of informed consent carefully. If you have any questions or difficulties in understanding, please contact me via chaskelr@hu-berlin.de. Responses are processed in accordance with the information obligations pursuant to Art. 13 EU GDPR.

I do not collect email-addresses, names, or IP addresses. I do not store cookies on participants’ devices. I do collect the time that participants start the experiment and end the experiment. Participants are free to skip questions related to demographic information (most importantly age group and gender).

Data will first be pseudonymized, the anonymised.
